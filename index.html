01 — Homepage
Homepage CRO

The homepage is the primary landing page for cold traffic, paid ads, and branded search. Currently, it has no hero CTA, no visible trust signals, and zero reviews — all of which are directly costing conversions before a visitor even reaches a product page.

🚨
No CTA in hero — full-screen video with zero direction for visitors
CRITICAL · HIGH IMPACT

The homepage hero is a full-screen background video with no overlaid headline, no sub-copy, and no call-to-action button. Visitors who land here without prior brand awareness have no immediate direction and no reason to scroll. This is especially damaging for paid traffic — money spent on ads sending people to a page with no CTA is wasted.

Fix Overlay the hero with: (1) a short punchy H1 e.g. "Ancient Botanicals. Modern Purity."; (2) a one-line subheadline; (3) dual CTAs — "Shop Shilajit" (primary gold button) + "Explore All Products" (secondary outline); (4) 3–4 trust chips directly in the hero area.
🚨
Lab certificates — Solvara's #1 differentiator — buried in a dropdown
CRITICAL · HIGH IMPACT

Third-party UK lab-tested certificates are only accessible via the "Authenticity" navigation dropdown. For a supplement brand competing against Amazon and Holland & Barrett, lab certification transparency is the single strongest trust signal available. It should appear in the first scroll — not hidden in a nav item.

Fix Add a full-width trust bar immediately below the hero (or within it): "✓ UK Lab Certified   ✓ Third-Party Tested   ✓ Himalayan Sourced   ✓ Free UK Delivery £59.99+". Gold background, dark text — unmissable.
🚨
No reviews or social proof anywhere on the homepage
CRITICAL · HIGH IMPACT

The /pages/our-customers page exists but testimonials, star ratings, and review counts are nowhere visible on the homepage or product cards. For a health supplement brand, social proof is one of the top three conversion drivers — particularly important for a new brand that visitors haven't heard of before.

Fix Add a reviews carousel section below the featured products. Install Judge.me (free Shopify app). Display star ratings + review counts on every product card in the featured products grid.
⚠️
Hero video is 16.9 MB — unacceptable for mobile visitors
HIGH IMPACT

Lighthouse reports a total homepage page weight of 16.9 MB, driven by an uncompressed hero video. On mobile (the majority of your traffic), this means a 10–15 second blank screen before the hero loads. On mobile connections, the site is effectively broken before a visitor sees a single product.

Fix Compress the hero video to WebM under 3 MB using HandBrake or Cloudflare Stream. Serve a static image on mobile using Shopify's responsive section settings. Target total page weight under 3 MB.
⚠️
No incentive in the email capture — plain "Stay in the loop" in footer
HIGH IMPACT

The newsletter signup sits in the footer with zero incentive. Email captures without a compelling lead magnet or offer typically convert at under 0.5%. For a wellness brand, a downloadable guide (e.g. "The Beginner's Guide to Ayurvedic Wellness") or a discount code will dramatically increase signup rates.

Fix Move a more prominent email capture to mid-page. Offer: "Download free: The Solvara Guide to Ayurvedic Botanicals" or "Get 10% off your first order". Target 3–5% conversion rate on email capture.

02 — Product Page (PDP)
Product Page CRO

The product page is where the purchase decision is made. Currently, every product page is missing the core conversion elements: reviews, a satisfaction guarantee, a subscribe option, and any urgency signals near the Add to Cart button.

🚨
Zero reviews on all product pages — the #1 conversion killer for supplement brands
CRITICAL · HIGH IMPACT

There are zero reviews displayed on any product page. No star ratings, no review count, no customer quotes. For health supplements, social proof before purchasing is non-negotiable — customers need third-party validation before ingesting something. Studies show health supplement pages with 20+ reviews convert at 2–3× the rate of pages without.

Fix Install Judge.me (free Shopify app). Email all past customers for reviews immediately. Display star rating average + review count in the product header, below the H1. Target 20+ reviews per product within 30 days. This is the single biggest conversion lever on the site.
🚨
No guarantee, trust signals, or shipping callout near the Add to Cart button
CRITICAL · HIGH IMPACT

The ATC button exists in isolation with zero surrounding conversion elements. No "In Stock" indicator, no satisfaction guarantee badge, no free delivery callout, no social proof. The micro-copy immediately surrounding an ATC button is one of the highest-impact CRO opportunities on any ecommerce page.

Fix Add three elements below the ATC button: (1) 🔒 "30-Day Satisfaction Guarantee — cancel anytime"; (2) "✓ In Stock — dispatched within 24hrs"; (3) "🚚 Free UK delivery on orders over £59.99". Takes 30 minutes to implement, measurably lifts conversion.
🚨
No Subscribe & Save option visible in the UI — major LTV opportunity missed
CRITICAL · HIGH IMPACT

The page code references subscription functionality but there is no visible Subscribe & Save toggle in the product UI. Shilajit and Ashwagandha are daily supplements — subscription revenue is transformational for a brand at this stage. Getting even 30% of orders on subscription dramatically changes the unit economics.

Fix Add a one-time vs Subscribe & Save toggle above the ATC button. Show the price saving clearly — e.g. "Subscribe & Save 15% → £50.99/mo". Use Shopify native subscriptions or Recharge. The toggle should be visible without scrolling.
🚨
Placeholder FAQ text is live on all product pages — actively damaging credibility
CRITICAL · URGENT

Both the Shilajit and Ashwagandha product pages contain an accordion labelled "Need Help?" with entries reading: "Use this text to answer questions in as much detail as possible for your customers." This template placeholder text is live, visible to every visitor, and signals that the site is unfinished. Any visitor who reaches this section will lose confidence immediately.

Fix Replace with 4–6 real, keyword-rich FAQs per product. For Shilajit: "How do I use Shilajit Resin?", "What's the difference between resin and powder?", "Is Shilajit legal in the UK?", "How long until I see results?", "What is the recommended daily dose?". For Ashwagandha: "Can I take Ashwagandha daily?", "Is it safe during pregnancy?", etc.
⚠️
"Pairs Well With" cross-sell section exists but appears empty
HIGH IMPACT

A "Pairs well with" section is present in the product page template but either renders empty or shows no products. Cross-selling is one of the highest-ROI ecommerce tactics — it costs nothing and lifts average order value by 10–20%.

Fix Set up cross-sell logic: Shilajit → suggest Ashwagandha + Brass Spoon. Ashwagandha → suggest Shatavari (for women) or Shilajit. Moringa → suggest Ashwagandha. Implement via Shopify's "Frequently Bought Together" or a simple manual product recommendation block.
⚠️
Klarna available but not promoted — £59.99 Shilajit price is a barrier
HIGH IMPACT

Klarna is available at checkout (shown in footer icons) but not mentioned anywhere on the product page near the price. At £59.99 for the Shilajit, a visible "or 3 payments of £20.00 with Klarna" callout removes a significant price friction point for new customers who haven't tried the product before.

Fix Add a Klarna badge and payment split callout directly below the price: "or 3 x £20.00 with [Klarna logo]". Shopify has a native Klarna messaging widget for this — 5-minute implementation.

03 — Collection / Category Page
Collection Page CRO

Collection pages don't currently exist on solvara.co.uk — visitors go directly from homepage to individual product pages. This is both an SEO gap (no category-level keyword rankings) and a CRO gap (no browse layer for discovery). These recommendations cover what the collection pages should look like when built.

🚨
No collection pages exist — missing the entire category browse experience
CRITICAL · BUILD IMMEDIATELY

The site structure goes homepage → individual product pages with no category layer. This means: no ranking opportunity for category-level commercial terms ("ayurvedic supplements uk", "adaptogens uk"), no browse experience for visitors exploring the range, and no landing page for broad paid campaigns beyond a single product.

Fix Create at minimum two Shopify collections: /collections/ayurvedic-supplements-uk and /collections/womens-wellness-supplements. Each needs: an H1, a 100–150 word keyword-rich description, product grid with reviews shown, and filter/sort options.
⚠️
Collection pages must have goal/benefit filters, not just product listing
HIGH IMPACT · BUILD RIGHT FIRST TIME

When building collection pages, the default Shopify layout (unfiltered product grid) misses a critical opportunity. Supplement buyers often browse by health goal, not by product name. A visitor who searches "adaptogens uk" wants to filter by "stress relief" or "energy" — not scroll through 7 products without context.

Fix Add filter by: Health Goal (Energy, Stress Relief, Women's Health, Digestion, Sleep). Sort by: Best Seller, Price Low-High, Newest. Add a short benefit-led intro paragraph above the grid. Display product cards with star ratings, key benefit callout, and price.
⚠️
Collection hero should position Solvara's sourcing story — not just list products
HIGH IMPACT

A collection page should do brand positioning work, not just display a product grid. The sourcing story (Himalayas, 18,000ft, UK lab-tested) is the core differentiator and should be present on every category page to prime visitors before they see a product.

Fix Collection hero: dark background with a brief positioning headline ("Himalayan Botanicals. Lab-Certified. Delivered to Your Door.") + trust bar + filter pills. This gives context and builds confidence before the product grid loads.

04 — Blog / Content Template
Blog Content CRO

No blog currently exists. These recommendations define how every blog article should be built from day one — as a conversion funnel that turns informational readers into product buyers, not just an article people read and leave.

🚨
Blog template must be built as a conversion funnel from the start
CRITICAL · BUILD RIGHT FIRST TIME

Most supplement brands build a blog as a "content" exercise without commercial architecture. Every article should have a clear conversion path: reader arrives via search → gets value from the article → is shown the relevant Solvara product → clicks through and buys. This doesn't happen without deliberate structural design in the template.

Required elements in every article
1. Sticky sidebar with relevant product callout (image + price + CTA)
2. Mid-article CTA block after the main benefits section
3. "Products mentioned in this article" cards at the bottom
4. Email capture offer (lead magnet or first-order discount)
5. Author bio with credentials (E-E-A-T signal for Google)
⚠️
Author bio is essential for health content E-E-A-T — Google requires demonstrated expertise
HIGH IMPACT · SEO + TRUST

Health supplement content falls under Google's YMYL (Your Money Your Life) guidelines. Articles without an identifiable author with health credentials will be deprioritised in rankings compared to articles that demonstrate genuine expertise. This is non-optional for the supplement niche.

Fix Every article must display an author bio with: photo, name, credentials ("Certified Nutritionist" / "MSc Herbal Medicine" / "Reviewed by [expert name]"). If Solvara doesn't have in-house experts, partner with a UK nutritionist or herbalist for a review/endorsement arrangement.
⚠️
Email capture within blog articles — owned audience is the highest-value asset
HIGH IMPACT

Blog traffic without an email capture mechanism is wasted. Visitors read the article and leave — no way to re-engage them. A mid-article email capture with a compelling lead magnet (free PDF guide, discount code) can convert 3–8% of blog readers into email subscribers who can be nurtured to purchase.

Fix Add a mid-article email capture box after the first major section: "Download free: The Solvara Guide to [topic]" or "Get 10% off your first order — join 2,400+ customers". This should be styled to match the article but visually distinct enough to stop the scroll.
💡
Related product sidebar should be sticky — keeps CTA visible throughout the entire read
MEDIUM IMPACT

A product callout in the sidebar that scrolls away as the reader progresses through a 2,000-word article is functionally useless. A sticky sidebar (position: sticky in CSS) that follows the reader down the page ensures the product CTA is always visible without being intrusive.

Fix Implement position: sticky; top: 80px; on the sidebar product widget. Show: product image, name, price, star rating, "Shop Now" button. Update the product shown based on which article the reader is on (e.g. shilajit article → sticky shilajit widget).
